The Assamese Silk Tradition

Understanding the heritage behind every thread

Muga Silk ‚Äî The Golden Treasure

Muga silk is exclusive to Assam and is renowned worldwide for its natural golden color and exceptional durability. This precious silk is produced by semi-domesticated silkworms that feed on som and sualu leaves found only in Assam. The resulting fabric has a distinctive golden sheen that actually grows richer and more lustrous with age, making Muga silk garments treasured heirlooms passed down through generations.

Pat Silk ‚Äî The Pristine Beauty

Pat silk, also known as Mulberry silk, is celebrated for its soft texture and lustrous white appearance. Assamese weavers create stunning designs on Pat silk using traditional techniques, incorporating vibrant colored motifs and intricate borders. The versatility of Pat silk makes it perfect for both traditional garments and contemporary designs, suitable for various occasions from formal events to cultural celebrations.

Eri Silk ‚Äî The Fabric of Peace

Eri silk, often called the "fabric of peace," is produced without harming the silkworms, making it a sustainable and ethical choice. Known for its thermal properties and soft, cotton-like texture, Eri silk is ideal for shawls and winter garments. It provides warmth without weight and has excellent moisture absorption properties, making it comfortable in various climates.
