The Assamese Silk Tradition

Understanding the heritage behind every thread

Muga Silk - The Golden Thread

Muga silk is exclusive to Assam and is known for its natural golden color and exceptional durability. This precious silk is produced by semi-domesticated silkworms that feed on som and sualu leaves. The resulting fabric has a distinctive golden sheen that grows richer with age.

Pat Silk - The Pristine Beauty

Pat silk, also known as Mulberry silk, is celebrated for its soft texture and lustrous white appearance. Assamese weavers create stunning designs on Pat silk, making it perfect for both traditional and contemporary garments.

Eri Silk - The Fabric of Peace

Eri silk, often called the "fabric of peace," is produced without harming the silkworms. Known for its thermal properties and soft texture, Eri silk is ideal for shawls and winter garments.

The Artisans Behind Our Products

Celebrating the skilled hands that create magic with every thread

Every garment in our collection is handwoven by master artisans who have dedicated their lives to perfecting the craft of silk weaving. These skilled weavers, often working from their homes in rural Assam, carry forward techniques that have been passed down through generations.

Each Sador Mekhela takes days, sometimes weeks, to complete. The intricate motifs, the careful selection of colors, and the precision of every thread reflect not just skill, but passion and cultural pride. When you wear our garments, you wear the artistry of Assamese master weavers.

We are proud to support these artisan communities and ensure their incredible work reaches customers who truly appreciate the value of authentic, handcrafted textiles.
